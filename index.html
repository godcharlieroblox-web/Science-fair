BAKING SODA VOLCANO — LIVE SIMULATION
Intensity
Speed

⚗️ Science Fact

Baking soda (NaHCO₃) and vinegar (acetic acid) neutralise each other, releasing CO₂ gas that creates thick foam oozing down the volcano sides.

NaHCO₃ + CH₃COOH → CO₂ + H₂O + CH₃COONa

📊 Stats

Reaction TypeACID-BASE
Gas ReleasedCO₂
Flow StyleOOZE DOWN
Danger LevelSAFE ✅

🧪 Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp baking soda
  • ½ cup white vinegar
  • A few drops dish soap
  • Food colouring (red/orange)
  • Volcano model / bottle
⚠️ Always perform real experiments with adult supervision.
MENTOS GEYSER — LIVE SIMULATION
Intensity
Speed

⚗️ Science Fact

Mentos candy has thousands of tiny pores. In Diet Coke, these act as nucleation sites for dissolved CO₂, causing an explosive chain reaction geyser.

CO₂(dissolved) → CO₂(gas) [nucleation]

📊 Stats

Reaction TypePHYSICAL
Gas ReleasedCO₂
Flow StyleTALL JET
HeightUP TO 9m

🧪 Ingredients

  • 2L bottle of Diet Coke
  • 1 roll of Mentos mints
  • Paper tube (delivery tool)
  • Open outdoor space!
⚠️ Do this OUTDOORS. Very messy — can spray 6–9 metres high.
ELEPHANT TOOTHPASTE — LIVE SIMULATION
Intensity
Speed

⚗️ Science Fact

Hydrogen peroxide rapidly decomposes into water and oxygen gas when catalysed by potassium iodide. The dish soap traps the O₂, creating a giant foam column.

2H₂O₂ → 2H₂O + O₂↑ (KI catalyst)

📊 Stats

Reaction TypeCATALYTIC
Gas ReleasedO₂
Flow StyleTHICK COLUMN
Danger LevelMODERATE ⚠️

🧪 Ingredients

  • ½ cup 6% hydrogen peroxide
  • 1 tsp potassium iodide in water
  • Squirt of dish soap
  • Food colouring
  • Large beaker or bottle
⚠️ H₂O₂ can burn skin. Wear gloves and goggles. Adult supervision required.

⚗️ Science Fact

Oil and water don't mix (density difference). Alka-Seltzer tablets react with water to produce CO₂ bubbles that carry coloured water droplets up through oil, then release and sink — creating lava lamp motion.

NaHCO₃ + citric acid → CO₂ + H₂O

📊 Stats

Reaction TypeACID-BASE
EffectBUOYANCY
Flow StyleFLOATING BLOBS
Danger LevelSAFE ✅

🧪 Ingredients

  • Tall glass or jar
  • Baby oil (¾ full)
  • Water + food colouring
  • Alka-Seltzer tablet
  • Flashlight (optional)
⚠️ Safe experiment! Great for kids with adult guidance.
DRY ICE FOG — LIVE SIMULATION
Density
Speed

⚗️ Science Fact

Dry ice is solid CO₂ at −78°C. When placed in warm water it sublimates (skips liquid phase) into CO₂ gas. The cold gas condenses surrounding water vapour into a dense, low-rolling white fog.

CO₂(solid) → CO₂(gas) [sublimation]

📊 Stats

Reaction TypeSUBLIMATION
Temp−78°C
Flow StyleROLLING CLOUD
Danger LevelCAUTION ⚠️

🧪 Ingredients

  • Dry ice pellets (purchased)
  • Bowl of warm water
  • Tongs for handling
  • Gloves (insulated)
⚠️ NEVER touch dry ice bare-handed — causes frostbite. Ensure ventilation. Adults only for handling dry ice.

⚗️ Science Fact

Similar to Mentos, adding table salt or Mentos to Diet Coke with aspartame and CO₂ under pressure causes rapid nucleation. The pressure release launches a powerful continuous jet.

CO₂(aq) + nucleation sites → CO₂(g) jet

Gas ReleasedCO₂
